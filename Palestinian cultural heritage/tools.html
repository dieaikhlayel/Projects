Tools as Cultural Artifacts

Traditional Palestinian tools offer insight into the daily lives and livelihoods of past generations. Many of these implements were handmade by local craftspeople using materials available in their environment, reflecting both practical needs and cultural values.

Agricultural tools dominate Palestinian traditional tool collections, highlighting the central role of farming in Palestinian society. Olive wood and stone were commonly used materials, while metal tools were often forged by village blacksmiths. Many traditional tools continue to be used in rural areas, serving as a living connection to Palestinian heritage.
